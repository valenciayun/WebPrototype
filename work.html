The Importance of Early Education

How does early education impact children in the long-term?


Early education impacts the development of a child's skills and abilities. It is responsible for developing key social and emotional coping skills, which become crucial for the child's future educational career. Furthermore, it develops a sense of independence and curiosity for children as they grow in their love for learning and readiness to learn in school ("5 Reasons Why Early Childhood Development Matters", 2018).

How can we help children who need special needs or are disadvantaged?


Disadvantaged children are behind their classmates particularly in their vocabularies and comprehension skills. By age six, many are seven times more likely to be performing poorly at school ("Literacy Programs", n.d.). Thus, children with disadvantages need help in laying a strong foundation for their early childhood development through high quality early childhood education. This will help them to develop their cognitive abilities, which will enhance their social and emotional skills as they interact with their peers ("Early Education Matters", 2020).
